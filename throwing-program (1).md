# Throwing Program: 65 → 80-85 mph

**Timeline:** 14–15 months (now through summer 2027)
**Starting point:** 65 mph, under 2 years experience

## PRE-THROW WARM-UP (every throw session, 10 min)

| Exercise | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Band pull-aparts | 2 × 15 |
| Band external rotation | 2 × 12/arm |
| Band internal rotation | 2 × 12/arm |
| Wrist circles + flexion/extension stretches | 1 min |
| Easy catch play throws, 30 ft → 60 ft | 10–15 (don't count) |

---

## JAVELIN (Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday — before long toss)

| Drill | Reps | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Standing throws | 10 | Sideways to target, step and throw. Full body, ground up. Zero arm effort. If javelin wobbles, sequencing was wrong. |
| Walk-in throws | 10 | Three-step approach. Should feel effortless if sequencing is right. |
| Full run-up throws | 5–10 | Full approach, max distance. Everything from the ground up has to work in sequence. |

**Equipment:** Lightweight training javelin (300–400g) or broomstick with tape on one end.
**If no javelin:** 20–30 weighted ball throws (7–10 oz) with full delivery.

---

## LONG TOSS (Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday — after javelin)

Build to max distance on a line — no arcing the ball. When you can't keep it on a line, that's your current max. Stay there a few throws, then compress back in.

**Throws:** 30–50 working throws (builds over time)

**Distance targets:**

| Period | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Month 1 | Find baseline (probably 40–55m) |
| Month 3 | 55–70m |
| Month 6 | 70–85m |
| Month 9+ | 85–100m+ |

Long toss distance is your velocity indicator. As distance goes up, velocity follows. Track it.

---

## MOUND / BULLPEN (Wednesday, Friday, Sunday)

**Throws:** 40–50 working throws
**Intensity:** Mix. Mostly 70–85%, with some higher intent throws mixed in.
**Film:** Once a week, side angle and behind angle.

No command work for the first 5–6 months. Let the delivery develop and stabilize first.

### Command Development (Month 6+)

When velocity is climbing and the delivery is consistent:

- Pick two locations per session, work in blocks of 5
- Track strike percentage — target 60%+ before worrying about quadrant precision
- Simulated counts: 0-0, 1-1, 0-2, 3-1 situations

---

## POST-THROW RECOVERY (every throw session, 15 min)

| Exercise | Target | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Cool-down lob throws | 10–15 | Easy throws at 30–40 ft, getting progressively lighter. |
| Reverse throws | 10 | Face away from target, throw light ball backwards over head. Activates decelerators. |
| Foam roller lat | 60s | Lie on side, roller under armpit/lat. Roll slowly from armpit to mid-ribcage. |
| Lacrosse ball posterior shoulder | 60–90s | Ball against wall behind shoulder (behind armpit area). Lean in, find tender spots, hold. |
| Lacrosse ball pec minor | 60s | Ball against wall just below collarbone near front of shoulder. Lean in, move slowly. |
| Forearm flexor work | 60s | Lacrosse ball on table or opposite thumb along inside of forearm, elbow to wrist. |
| Sleeper stretch | 30s/side | Lie on throwing side, shoulder at 90° in front, elbow bent 90°. Push hand toward floor. |
| Cross-body stretch | 30s/side | Pull throwing arm across chest with opposite hand. |
| Wrist flexor stretch | 30s | Arm extended, palm facing away, fingers up. Pull fingers back toward you. |
| Wrist extensor stretch | 30s | Arm extended, palm down. Press back of hand down with other hand. |

## VOLUME RAMP-UP

| Period | Weekly Throwing Volume | Notes |
|--------|----------------------|-------|
| Weeks 1–4 | 150–180 throws | Build the daily habit. Consistency over volume. |
| Weeks 5–8 | 180–220 throws | Arm adapting. Full session volumes. |
| Weeks 9–12 | 220–260 throws | Full training volume. Deload end of week 12. |
| Month 4+ | 220–280 throws | Steady state. Deload every 4th week. |

**Deload every 4th week:** Drop throwing volume 30–40%. 

---

## RULES

1. Post-throw recovery after every throw session. Non-negotiable.
2. Monday is rest from throwing.
3. Deload every 4th week — drop throwing 30–40%.
4. Film weekly. Side and behind.
5. Long toss distance is your velocity indicator. Track it.
6. No command work until month 6+.
7. If something hurts, stop and figure out why. 15 months is a long runway — don't waste it on an injury.
